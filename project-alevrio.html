Alervio: Nutrition Standards and Menu Data Intelligence

Built clinically grounded nutrition standards and improved menu-data quality to support dietary intelligence, including allergy and nutrition use cases.

Project Facts

  • Organization: Alervio
  • Role: Clinical Data Research Intern (remote)
  • Focus: Clinical and nutrition standards plus structured menu data for AI systems
  • Context: Dietary intelligence for food and hospitality workflows

Note: This page summarizes my contribution as an intern. Product direction, engineering ownership, and official claims about model performance are maintained by Alervio and its leadership.

Overview

Alervio positions itself as an intelligence layer that transforms menus, recipes, and dietary profiles into usable, continuously updated dietary intelligence for operators and guests.

My work focused on two complementary needs: (1) scientifically defensible nutrition and clinical reference standards, and (2) clean, structured menu inputs that downstream systems can reliably interpret.

My Contribution

  • Clinical and nutrition dataset building: Researched and extracted 200+ clinical and nutritional datasets from literature, guidelines, and medical databases to support model development.
  • Standards grounding: Helped establish fundamental medical and nutrition guidelines using 10+ major datasets so recommendations remained clinically relevant.
  • Reference values and documentation: Defined baseline "normal adult" values (example: daily sodium intake) and documented how targets shift under common conditions where clinical guidance differs (example: hypertension, kidney disease), with clear provenance back to guideline sources.
  • Menu data cleaning and structuring: Transitioned to the data team to improve menu-quality inputs by cleaning large volumes of menu data using LLM-assisted extraction plus web scraping and PDF scraping workflows.
  • Cross-functional collaboration: Worked with AI engineers and stakeholders to identify data gaps and implement fixes that improved recommendation accuracy.

What I Built, In Practice

  • Standards library: A structured set of reference values and rules (nutrients, contraindications, condition-specific adjustments), designed for traceability and easy updating as guidelines change.
  • Data quality workflow: A repeatable process for turning messy menus (web pages, PDFs, mixed formatting) into consistent structured fields like ingredients, allergens, nutrition attributes, and serving sizes.
  • QA checks: Spot checks and validation heuristics to catch issues like unit mismatches, duplicate items, partial ingredient lists, and inconsistent naming that can break downstream dietary logic.

Skills Developed

  • Clinical data provenance: Turning guideline text into auditable, machine-usable standards.
  • Information extraction: Web scraping plus PDF scraping for semi-structured menu content.
  • LLM-assisted data ops: Using model outputs as a draft extractor, then validating and normalizing into schemas.
  • Applied nutrition context: Reasoning about baseline intake targets vs condition-adjusted guidance.
