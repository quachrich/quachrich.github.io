Public Health Outreach 4 months (COVID-19 vaccine equity programming)

COVID-19 Vaccine Hesitancy and Vaccine Equity

Community-facing vaccine outreach and multilingual resource support through Philadelphia Chinatown Development Corporation, focused on reducing barriers to vaccination and building trust in immigrant and limited-English-proficiency communities.

Project facts

  • Organization: Philadelphia Chinatown Development Corporation (PCDC)
  • Location: Chinatown, Philadelphia
  • Program focus: Vaccine equity, hesitancy reduction, multilingual access to COVID-19 resources
  • Service model: Community education plus direct vaccination access and navigation support

Note: This page summarizes my contribution during a 4-month role. Program leadership, clinical operations, and official reporting are managed by PCDC and its public health partners.

Overview

PCDC’s COVID-19 resources emphasize practical barrier removal: helping residents retrieve immunization records, navigate vaccination logistics, and access language-appropriate information. PCDC also supported community-facing messaging and maintained a directory of vaccination sites with details like language accessibility and hours of operation.

In addition to resource navigation, PCDC has offered recurring COVID-19 vaccinations at the Crane Community Center and published outreach materials to encourage vaccination uptake.

Vaccine hesitancy approach

The program treated hesitancy as a trust and access problem, not just an information deficit. The work combined culturally tailored messaging with concrete next steps, including where to get vaccinated, how to find language-accessible sites, and how to replace lost vaccine cards through official record requests.

  • Access-first design: Reduce friction by pairing clear guidance with direct links, forms, and contact points.
  • Language accessibility: Provide bilingual or multilingual materials so residents can act without intermediaries.
  • Community narrative: Use values-based messaging that connects vaccination to protecting family and community.

My contribution

  • Resource support: Helped residents navigate COVID-19 vaccine resources and administrative steps, including record retrieval workflows.
  • Outreach execution: Supported community-facing vaccine encouragement activities aligned with PCDC’s public campaign materials.
  • On-the-ground operations: Assisted across program needs during a high-throughput period for vaccine equity work.
  • Direct impact: Contributed to vaccination delivery efforts totaling 1,000+ people during my 4-month tenure (my role contribution estimate).

The 1,000+ figure reflects the vaccination throughput I supported during my time in the program. For official totals and reporting, refer to PCDC and its clinical partners.

Program components reflected on the PCDC resource page

  • Immunization record retrieval: Step-by-step guidance for requesting vaccine records through city and state pathways.
  • Community campaign: “Love your family, get vaccinated!” messaging designed to address stigma and build motivation.
  • Vaccination access: Weekly vaccination availability at Crane Community Center and a citywide site directory with language access details.
